About the Ashram

Dinod Dham —
Where Souls Find Rest

Radhaswami Satsang Dinod (RSSD) is a non-profit spiritual organisation seated in the peaceful village of Dinod, 11 km from Bhiwani and 130 km from Delhi. Established in the latter decades of the twentieth century, it has grown into a beloved centre for seekers of truth.

The ashram was founded by Param Sant Tara Chand Ji Maharaj, born in 1925 in Dinod. He dedicated his entire life — unmarried, austere, and devoted — to finding eternal peace. Inspired by the great saints before him, he declared that inner liberation is attainable in one's lifetime.

Today, under the divine guidance of Sant Kanwar Saheb Ji Maharaj, the satsang continues to guide thousands on the path of Surat Shabd Yoga.

The Radhaswami Faith

Three Pillars of the Path

The Radhaswami faith offers the simplest and most direct way to achieve eternal peace within this very lifetime.

🕉️

Naam — The Divine Word

Naam is the sacred inner sound current — the cosmic vibration that connects the soul to its divine source. Initiation into Naam is the first step on the spiritual path, given by the living Master.

Learn More →
🙏

Seva — Selfless Service

Seva means performing service without ego or expectation of reward. It purifies the mind and opens the heart, making one worthy of higher spiritual states through dedicated, humble action.

Learn More →
🧘

Simran — Remembrance

Simran is the continuous repetition and remembrance of the divine names. Through Simran, the wandering mind is stilled, and the practitioner is drawn inward toward the light and sound within.

The Star Monument

The Star Monument is one of the most breathtaking buildings in India. It houses the holy samadhi of Param Sant Huzur Tara Chand Ji Maharaj, fondly known as ‘Bade Maharaj Ji’ by his followers. The monument is situated within the Radhaswami Satsang Bhawan Complex at Dinod, approximately 11 kilometres from Bhiwani, Haryana.

Its foundation was laid on 1st October 1997 by Param Sant Huzur Kanwar Ji Maharaj, and by his blessings, the construction was completed in just four years. The monument takes the form of a hexagonal pyramid, its six sides shaped like a star — a tribute to Maharaj Ji’s name, ‘Tara’, which means star. Each side measures approximately 101 feet from the base, which rests on a raised platform of about 6 feet. Three alternating sides are finished in white marble, while the other three are clad in blue Italian glass, capable of withstanding extreme climatic conditions. Remarkably, the structure stands entirely without pillars or columns.

The exterior walls bear prominent stars, while the interior of the samadhi is adorned with countless sparkling stars, evoking the divine glory of Param Sant Huzur Tara Chand Ji Maharaj. Ventilation is achieved through a traditional and elegant system — fresh air passes over fountains, cools naturally, enters through lower openings, and is expelled through vents at the top. Exhaust fans supplement this during humid seasons.

The samadhi symbolises the timeless Sanskrit invocation:

“Tamso ma jyotirgamay — Asato ma sadgamay — Mrityorma amritam gamay”
(Lead us from darkness to light — from untruth to truth — from death to immortality.)

Inside, the samadhi is built on a rectangular platform at the centre. A statue of Maharaj Ji stands near the western wall, and six paintings depicting milestones from his life adorn the six corners. Marble slippers placed upon a lotus flower near the samadhi represent the sacred feet of Bade Maharaj Ji.

The surrounding garden is a world of its own — lush green lawns, vibrant flowers, marble pathways in milky white, black, and yellow, and five fountains are spread gracefully around the samadhi. Stars are etched into the boundary wall, each with a moon engraved at its centre, reflecting Maharaj Ji’s name. Every evening, the gardens are illuminated in a magnificent display of lights and fountains, earning the place the title of a ‘celestial garden’.

Within the garden, a small hut on the right — Bade Maharaj Ji’s personal place of meditation — holds deep spiritual significance. To the left lies a holy well, whose water is believed by devoted followers to offer relief from many ailments. A man-made waterfall in the north-eastern corner, fashioned from mountain-shaped stones, adds to the serene and mesmerising atmosphere of the complex.

The three ornate gateways, the vast star-shaped white structure, the celestial garden, the sacred hut, the holy well, and the flowing waterfall together create a place of unparalleled divine beauty — a true paradise on earth.
